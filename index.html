Legal

The Canadian Aviation Regulations (CARs) require unmanned air vehicle (UAV) operators to apply for a Special Flight Operations Certificates (SFOCs) so Transport Canada can ensure operators use their UAV reliably and safely. These certificates are required when drone operators do not plan on operating in a Class G airspace and can take up to 22 business days to process. After a number of successful applications Transport Canada may grant pilots a Standing “Blanket” SFOC in which all flights within a given area granted permission without the need to process individual SFOC applications.

At this point in time Perspective Aerial Media has not yet been granted a Standing SFOC and therefore must adhere to the CARs individual SFOC 22-day process. We apologize for the inconvenience and hope to be able to provide a faster and more convenient service in the near future.

Please check the Airspace map to see if your location falls within a class G airspace and is therefore exempt from the 22 day SFOC application process.
